Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how League Matchup Analyzer (the “Service”) handles information. The Service is designed around data minimization: it does not require user accounts, does not ask visitors to submit a Riot ID, and does not build individual player profiles.

1. Information Processed from Riot Games

The data pipeline processes information made available through authorized Riot Games developer services, including Match-V5, League-V4, and Data Dragon. Depending on the source response, processed fields may include match identifiers, queue and patch information, timestamps, champion and role selections, match statistics, objectives, outcomes, and Riot-issued technical identifiers.

Riot-issued identifiers are handled only where technically necessary for retrieval, deduplication, pipeline integrity, and reproducibility. They are not displayed in the public analytics interface, are not combined with external identity information, and are not used to identify a real-world person.

2. Information Collected from Website Visitors

The public application does not require registration and does not intentionally collect names, email addresses, payment information, passwords, Riot account credentials, or user-submitted match histories.

The Service does not deploy advertising trackers or behavioral analytics cookies. Standard technical logs may nevertheless be generated by hosting, networking, or security providers when a visitor accesses the site. Such logs may include an IP address, user agent, requested URL, timestamp, and security event information. They are used only for delivery, reliability, abuse prevention, and incident investigation.

3. How Information Is Used

Information is processed only to:

  • retrieve and validate authorized League of Legends match data;
  • deduplicate matches and avoid unnecessary Riot API requests;
  • calculate aggregated champion, matchup, role, and composition statistics;
  • display statistical reliability, missing-data warnings, and methodology-aware visualizations;
  • maintain security, availability, auditability, and data quality; and
  • comply with applicable law and Riot Games developer requirements.

4. Aggregation and Public Display

Public-facing outputs are derived and aggregated. The Service is designed to display champion-level, role-level, matchup-level, and composition-level findings rather than raw individual match histories or player-level dossiers. Small samples and unavailable matchups are identified rather than replaced with fabricated estimates.

5. Storage and Retention

Match data and derived statistics may be stored in schema-controlled analytical formats, including Parquet, to support reproducible calculations, incremental updates, deduplication, and recovery from interrupted jobs. Retention is limited to what is reasonably necessary for those purposes, service operation, data-quality checks, and compliance obligations.

The project does not maintain behavioral advertising profiles or a database of visitor accounts. Operational logs are retained only as long as reasonably necessary for security, troubleshooting, and infrastructure administration.

6. Data Sharing and Sale

The project does not sell personal information, Riot API data, or access to the Riot Games API. It does not license or redistribute raw Riot API responses as a standalone dataset.

Limited information may be processed by infrastructure providers solely to host, secure, or deliver the Service. Information may also be disclosed when required by law, necessary to protect the Service or its users, or required to investigate abuse or a security incident.

7. Security

API requests are performed server-side over HTTPS. API credentials are kept outside public client code and are restricted to the registered product. The ingestion workflow uses controlled concurrency, rate limiting, caching, deduplication, checkpointing, and error handling to reduce unnecessary requests and operational risk.

No system can guarantee absolute security. Reasonable technical and organizational measures are used to protect credentials, stored data, and infrastructure, and these controls are reviewed when the application or applicable requirements change.

8. Cookies and Similar Technologies

The current Service does not use cookies for advertising, cross-site tracking, or behavioral profiling. If optional analytics or other cookie-based features are introduced in the future, this Policy and any required consent mechanism will be updated before those features are enabled.

9. Third-Party Services

The Service relies on Riot Games developer services and may rely on hosting, content-delivery, or security providers. Those third parties process information under their own terms and privacy notices. Requests concerning data held directly by Riot Games should be directed to Riot Games through its official privacy channels.

10. Children

The Service is a general-audience statistical research tool and is not directed to children. It does not knowingly solicit personal information from children.

11. Privacy Rights and Requests

Depending on applicable law, individuals may have rights concerning personal information processed by the Service, including rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, or objection. Because the public interface does not create user accounts or expose Riot-issued identifiers, the project may need reasonable information to verify and locate any relevant record without collecting excessive additional data.

Privacy or data-handling questions may be submitted through the official contact channel associated with the project's verified domain or Riot Developer Portal application.

12. Changes to This Policy

This Policy may be updated when the Service, data sources, infrastructure, legal requirements, or Riot Games policies change. The “Last updated” date identifies the current version.
